Abstract

Energetic frustration, characterized by conflicting local interactions, is a key determinant of protein dynamics, allostery, protein-protein interactions, enzymatic catalysis, and overall protein function. Although the principle of minimal frustration drives the global minimization of energetic conflicts for overall protein folding, local violations are selectively embedded to encode the complex energy landscapes necessary for these functions.

However, the immense computational cost of traditional deep mutational single-residue frustration analysis has prevented its application at the proteome scale, creating a significant bottleneck in structural biology. Here, we introduce FrustraMPNN, a message-passing neural network retrained via transfer learning that predicts a complete per-residue frustration-index mutation profile orders of magnitude faster than existing methods while maintaining high accuracy.

We validate FrustraMPNN on a diverse external set of over 3,300 human protein structures, achieving a Spearman correlation of up to 0.80 across all frustration categories, demonstrating robust generalizability. By conducting a thorough dataset ablation study, we find that the model's performance improves when trained on a broader range of protein sizes, highlighting an inherent limitation of datasets like Megascale.

We provide FrustraMPNN as an open-source tool, expecting that this model will enable exploration of new hypotheses in fields such as personalized structural biology, the local energetic frustration patterns involved in protein function, and proteomics at an unprecedented scale.

What is Local Energetic Frustration?

Local energetic frustration describes regions in proteins where amino acid interactions are not optimally satisfied. In the native state, most interactions are optimized to maintain structural stability (minimally frustrated). However, specific areas with conflicting interactions create local energetic tensions (highly frustrated).

Rather than being structural defects, these locally frustrated regions are often evolutionarily selected features critical for biological function:

  • Enzyme catalysis: Active site residues in energetically unfavorable conformations necessary for chemical reactions
  • Allostery: Frustration patterns drive protein dynamics essential for allosteric regulation
  • Protein-protein interactions: Conformational flexibility at interfaces mediated by frustration
  • Viral life cycles: Frustration governs conformational switches in viral proteins

Frustration Categories

Highly frustrated (index ≤ −1.0)
Conflicting interactions, often functional
Neutral (−1.0 < index < 0.58)
Average interactions
Minimally frustrated (index ≥ 0.58)
Optimized interactions, stable

Results

Computational Acceleration

FrustraMPNN achieves orders-of-magnitude speedup over existing physics-based methods. For single-residue frustration analysis, which requires evaluating all 20 amino acids at each position, the acceleration is particularly significant.

Protein Size FrustraMPNN (GPU) FrustratometeR (1 CPU) Speedup
100 residues ~20 ms ~3.4 min 1,200×
300 residues ~30 s ~19 h 2,300×
500 residues ~30 s ~35 h 4,500×

As an exemplar case, we analyzed the complete E. coli proteome (~4,300 proteins) in single-residue mode, which would require approximately 1.7 CPU-years with FrustratometeR, but can be completed in less than 12 GPU-hours with FrustraMPNN—a 7,000-fold reduction in computational time.

Model Performance

We validated FrustraMPNN on an external dataset of 3,921 human protein structures from the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database. The model trained on the balanced FireProt dataset with ESM embeddings and light attention achieved the best generalization performance.

Enzyme Active Site Validation

We validated FrustraMPNN predictions against FrustratometeR calculations across three representative enzymes: Class A beta-lactamase (PDB: 4BLM), Serine-carboxyl proteinase (PDB: 1NLU), and Beta-glucosidase (PDB: 1CBG). FrustraMPNN accurately recapitulates the global frustration landscapes, particularly preserving the high-frustration signals characteristic of catalytic sites.

For beta-lactamase, the highly frustrated key residues Lys73, Glu166, and Lys234, together with the neutrally frustrated Ser70, Ser130, and Ala237, were correctly identified by both FrustratometeR calculation and FrustraMPNN prediction.

Methods

Architecture

FrustraMPNN employs a graph-based neural network architecture specifically designed to capture the local structural context that determines local energetic frustration. Proteins are modeled as graphs, with each residue as a node and edges connecting spatially proximate 48-residue neighbors.

  1. Structure Encoder: Input backbone coordinates (N, Cα, C) are converted into pairwise distances and processed to capture geometric constraints
  2. Sequence Decoder: Conditions structural features on the wild-type sequence embedding
  3. Light Attention Block: Assigns weights to different features, enabling the model to focus on the most informative structural characteristics
  4. MLP Head: Two hidden layers map the learned representations to continuous frustration indices for each residue
  5. ESM Embeddings (optional): Evolutionary information from ESM-2 language model can be concatenated to improve predictions

Training Data

We trained FrustraMPNN using two distinct datasets:

  • FireProt: 3,438 experimentally characterized stability mutations from 100 natural proteins (45–495 residues), representing diverse folds and functions
  • Megascale: 272,712 systematic mutational scans of natural and de novo designed proteins, primarily under 75 residues

The superior generalization of the FireProt-trained model demonstrates that structural diversity in training data outweighs sheer data volume—a key finding for biological machine learning.

Quick Start

from frustrampnn import FrustraMPNN

# Load model
model = FrustraMPNN.from_pretrained("checkpoint.ckpt")

# Predict frustration
results = model.predict("protein.pdb")

# View results
print(results.head())
